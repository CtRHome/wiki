As of 2026, **fandom.com** is ranked as the **40th** most visited website in the world[^1]. For a long time, it was *one of the most accessible* platforms for creating fan wikis for games, comics, and more.

> "Why not Fandom? What changed?"

If you're not aware of shifts in the wiki ecosystem, since the start of the pandemic, Fandom has been *aggressively* pushing advertisements and has even attempted to incorporate **AI-generated content** on several occasions. Combined with other controversies, these changes have made it *increasingly difficult* for users to browse the site and enraged most wiki communities.

Despite many popular wikis attempting to move away from Fandom, the company has made significant efforts to retain them by **removing wiki access** from admins who attempt to add banners announcing a move to a new platform. Most of the time, Fandom's version of a wiki continues to rank above the newer wikis in Google search results. As a result, *outdated and unmoderated Fandom pages* often appear first, which directly contributes to the spread of **misinformation**[^2] and **spam**[^3].

Although the current *Cut the Rope* wiki on Fandom is an *exception* in some ways, and does contain some important information about Cut the Rope, its focus has long shifted almost entirely towards **[[Om Nom Stories]]**, catering to a younger userbase. As a result, it has become more of an "*Om Nom Wiki*" rather than an actual *Cut the Rope Wiki*. This site aims to finally create a hub for the real franchise, even if much smaller and nerdier.

> "Okay, but why not *anything* else in this case? Why GitHub?"

This wiki might embed clearly **copyrighted material**, and some content may involve ethically questionable practices such as **decompiling**. While some assets qualify as *fair use*, wiki farms like [Miraheze](https://miraheze.org) and [wiki.gg](https://www.wiki.gg) strictly prohibit hosting such material regardless.

For that reason, the exotic approach of using **GitHub Pages** was adopted - it is just as customizable as running MediaWiki software (if not even more), with the additional benefit that anything can be hosted here, so long as it doesn't blatantly violate GitHub's own Terms of Service. It's also fully static, so we don't have to worry about keeping servers up and running!

Additionally, most of the people working on the wiki are already *familiar with GitHub*, meaning there are no nasty surprises waiting to be discovered.
